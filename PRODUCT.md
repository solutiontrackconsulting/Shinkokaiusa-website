# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two primary users:

1. **Japanese-American seniors (65+)** living in the US who are considering returning to Japan for elder care. They may have declining health, limited digital fluency in English, and deep emotional ties to Japanese culture, family, and language. They may be reading this site themselves or have it shown to them by family.

2. **Adult children (30s–50s)** of those seniors — often US-born or long-term US residents — coordinating the return on a parent's behalf. They may be bilingual or primarily English-speaking. They carry emotional weight (guilt, distance, love) and need both competence and compassion from a service provider.

Both groups are making high-stakes, emotionally charged decisions. Trust must be earned through care and clarity, not through sales tactics.

## Product Purpose

Shinkokai USA coordinates the return of Japanese seniors living in the US to Japan for elder care — handling logistics, legal paperwork, facility matching, and family communication, in close partnership with Shinkoufukushikai (伸こう福祉会), a Japanese social welfare organization.

The website is the primary trust-building and lead-generation surface. Success means a visitor fills out the consultation form or contacts the team directly.

## Brand Personality

Dignified, warm, precise.

Voice: Calm and expert. Never pushy. Speaks to both cultures with equal respect. The tone of a trusted advisor who has done this many times and understands the full weight of the decision.

Emotional goal: Visitors should feel understood, relieved, and in safe hands — not sold to.

## Anti-references

- Generic hospital and healthcare sites: stock-photo doctors, teal/white clinical aesthetic, callout boxes
- Corporate elder care chains (Brookdale, Sunrise Senior Living): polished but emotionally cold
- Immigration law firms: heavy serif, dark navy, authoritative but impersonal
- Any design that signals "this is a transaction, not a relationship"

## Design Principles

1. **Dignity in every detail.** Every visual choice communicates respect for the senior's journey and the family's emotional state. Nothing feels rushed, cheap, or transactional.
2. **Trust through restraint.** This is not a loud brand. Whitespace and quiet confidence earn trust faster than bold claims. Let the content carry the weight.
3. **Both languages, equal weight.** English and Japanese coexist as equals. Japanese text is not a footnote, a decoration, or an afterthought.
4. **Familiar, not foreign.** The aesthetic should feel natural to the audience — referencing Japanese welfare organization design without being performative or exotic.
5. **The family is the client.** Content and design decisions acknowledge that adult children often drive the search, while the senior's dignity remains central to every interaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Japanese text marked up with `lang="ja"`. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. One clear heading hierarchy per page. All form inputs have associated labels.
